Judo

Judo is a Japanese martial art and Olympic sport that focuses on grappling, throws, and submissions. It was founded in 1882 by Kanō Jigorō, who adapted techniques from traditional jiu-jitsu to create a system emphasizing maximum efficiency and mutual respect. The word "judo" translates to "gentle way", reflecting its philosophy of using an opponent’s force against them rather than relying on brute strength. In competition, the goal is to throw, pin, or submit an opponent using joint locks or chokeholds.
