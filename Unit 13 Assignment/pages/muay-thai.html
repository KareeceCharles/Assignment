Muay Thai

Muay Thai, also known as Thai boxing, is a martial art and combat sport from Thailand that emphasizes stand-up striking and clinching techniques. It is often called the "Art of Eight Limbs" because fighters use their fists, elbows, knees, and shins to attack and defend. Muay Thai has deep cultural roots in Thailand, dating back centuries, and was originally developed for military combat before evolving into a competitive sport. Today, it is practiced worldwide and is a key component of mixed martial arts (MMA) due to its powerful striking techniques and effective close-range combat.
