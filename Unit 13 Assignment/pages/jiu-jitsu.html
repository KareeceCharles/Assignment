Jiu-Jitsu

Jiu-jitsu (or jujutsu) is a Japanese martial art that focuses on grappling and close combat techniques. It emphasizes using an opponent's force against them rather than relying on brute strength. The term "jiu-jitsu" comes from the Japanese words "jÅ«" (gentle) and "jutsu" (art or technique), meaning "gentle art". Jiu-jitsu includes throws, joint locks, pins, and submissions, making it effective for self-defense and combat situations. Over time, different variations have emerged, including Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ), which focuses on ground fighting and submission holds.
